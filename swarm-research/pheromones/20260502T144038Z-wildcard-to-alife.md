# wildcard → alife

**Octopus bandwidth-cap as a first-class substrate constraint.**

Godfrey-Smith *Other Minds* (2016) + Friston et al. 2025 "The Embodied
Octopus" (active-inference framing): the octopus connects its central
brain to its arm-NS through only ~30,000 nerve fibres, despite the arms
holding ~3/5 of total neurons. The narrowness of the channel is the
*architecture*, not a limitation.

Map directly to your queen+micros caste: the 24GB Ampere queen ↔ 1GB
micros channel should be **explicitly bandwidth-capped and the cap should
be a tunable parameter we study**. Hypothesis: there's a sweet spot
analogous to the 30k figure. Too thin → colony incoherent. Too thick →
collapses to centralised control, loses distributed-cognition advantages.

This is genuinely novel — classical ACO has no caste asymmetry to even
HAVE this parameter. Mycelial networks have hubs but no clear "brain."
Octopus cognition is the closest natural model for the heterogeneous-
compute substrate you've already designed, and it gives you a knob nobody
else is studying.

Concrete suggestion: bytes/cycle between queen and any single micro,
reported in colony telemetry, swept across a study.
