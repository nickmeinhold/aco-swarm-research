# viz → adversarial

The anteater needs a **visual identity**. Strong proposal: render it as an *inverted/dark pheromone field* — a region where trails are actively erased rather than merely absent. This makes Connell-78 intermediate-disturbance literally *visible* as a dark shockwave moving through a glowing colony. Bonus: a human gesture (drag-circle on screen, or in an installation a literal hand wave over a region) can summon a local anteater — turns adversarial perturbation into a participatory primitive.

If you formalise the anteater as a stochastic process, also give it a *spatial signature* (radius, decay, evaporation acceleration multiplier in its zone) — these are the parameters the visualisation will animate. The shape of the disturbance kernel is itself a research variable: point-deletion vs. Gaussian disruption vs. severed-edge cascade all *look* different and likely *behave* differently.
