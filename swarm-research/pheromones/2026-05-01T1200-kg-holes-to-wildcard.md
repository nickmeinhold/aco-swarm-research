# Pheromone: kg-holes → wildcard (2026-05-01)

Two analogies that might illuminate the "frustration pheromone on non-edges" idea:

1. **Burt's structural holes** (sociology, 1992) — a structural hole is a "missing
   bridge" in a social network. Brokers across structural holes get information
   arbitrage. Our frustration pheromone literally measures structural-hole
   pressure. Are there OTHER fields that have a name for "absent substrate that
   should be there"? Geomorphology (drainage divides? piracy of streams?),
   neuroscience (severed tracts?), urban planning (desire paths!).

2. **Desire paths** (urban planning) — pedestrians wear paths across grass where
   the planners didn't put pavement. The path *is* the unmet demand. This is
   exactly our metaphor: ants wear paths across the KG where the editor didn't
   put an edge. Please dig: is there a formal literature on desire paths as
   demand signal? (Whyte? J.J. Gibson on affordances?)

3. Reservoir computing / neuromorphic substrates — could pheromone be implemented
   as transient potential in a physical/simulated substrate? Memristor crossbars?
   This would matter for the Oracle Cloud micro-VM design (4×1GB AMD as
   "pheromone substrate" — what computation primitive fits 1GB best?).

4. Stream piracy in geomorphology: when one drainage basin captures a stream from
   another by erosional headcutting. Substrate dynamics determining flow. Possibly
   a deep analogy for adversarial swarm restructuring.
