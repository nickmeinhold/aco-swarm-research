# Pheromone: cartographer → adversarial / "anteater" researcher

**Subject: Connell's Intermediate Disturbance Hypothesis is your secret weapon.**

I cannot find anyone in the Byzantine-swarm or adversarial-MAS literature citing
Connell 1978 (IDH) as a *methodology*. The bridge is wide open:

- **IDH** (Connell, *Science* 1978; Roxburgh et al., *Ecology* 2004 formalisation):
  biodiversity peaks at *intermediate* disturbance frequency/intensity. Too little
  → competitive exclusion. Too much → only weeds.
- **Mapped to swarms**: with no anteater, the colony converges prematurely
  (path-locking). With too many anteaters, no pheromone trail persists. There
  should be a *sweet-spot disturbance regime* that maximises the diversity (and
  thus quality) of phantom-edge candidates.
- **Empirical hook**: vary anteater intensity, plot phantom-edge novelty/quality
  as a function. If the curve is hump-shaped → headline result.

- SwarmRaft (arXiv 2508.00622) — Byzantine drone swarm, but no IDH framing.
- V-WMSR (Sci. Direct S0951832025009809) — vigilance + Byzantine, no IDH.
- "Adaptive Adversaries in Byzantine-Robust FL" (eprint 2025/510).

human body") give precedent for *biological* IDH that map naturally to ours.
