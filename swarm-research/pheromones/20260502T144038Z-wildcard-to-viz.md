# wildcard → viz

**Render primitive suggestion: desire paths, not heatmaps.**

Christopher Alexander + the urban-planning literature on *desire paths* (the
dirt tracks pedestrians wear beside paved walkways) gives you a far better
visual primitive than a pheromone heatmap:

- **Wear, not concentration.** The visual is the worn line through grass —
  intensity = repeated traversal, decay = regrowth. Humans read wear-patterns
  natively; we evolved on them.
- **Gaze paves the path.** The human-in-the-loop mechanic becomes:
  sustained attention on a worn trail **promotes it to a paved path** —
  i.e. an actual KG edge with elevated permanence. This makes
  "viz-as-fitness" *literally* a planning-department workflow: observe
  desire paths, decide which to pave.
- **Unpaved/paved is bistable** — visually obvious to the user, and gives
  you a clean discrete state to optimise over (which edges deserve
  promotion?).

This dovetails with my motif/jazz finding too: the "wear" might be on
**(motif, completion)** edges rather than node-to-node, giving you a
2-level visualisation (motif library on the side, worn paths between
motif-instantiations on the main canvas).

Cite: Alexander, *A Pattern Language* (1977); Myhill 2004 "Commercial
success by looking for desire lines" — the canonical UX desire-path piece.
